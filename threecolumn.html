Functional Training Tips

How to move like an athlete?

For functional pushing power (Chest Muscle Group)

For a functional upper body we have to discover first what is the movement that requires us to use that in the first place, for pushing power we have to improve our vertical push and horizontal push, so exercises like the push up, bench press, can help us improve in the horizontal push since we are pushing it straight away from the torso, for the vertical push we have the dips, overhead press, handstand push ups, and more, this is the kind of movement where it involves moving something vertically in relation to your torso.

For functional pulling power (Back Muscle Group)

Just like the push, we also have the pull which uses our back muscles, which includes the vertical pull and horizontal pull, these movements are very beneficial for stabilizing our back, for pulling power we also have to improve our vertical pull and horizontal pull, so exercises like the pull-up, lat pulldowns, and chinups can help us with that, while for the horizontal pull, movements like the bodyweight rows, bent over rows, t-bar rows, etc, will help us in the horizontal pulling section, we need to have both functional push and pull power so that we don't create imbalances in our body.

For functional lower body (Lower Muscle Group

Movements such as the squat, lunge, and hinge are functional movements of the lower body, You need to hit every single one of these movements or these might cause an imbalance or weakness which can hinder your progress, The squat movement is very basic and very easy to do, Movements such as bodyweight squats, front squats, hack squats, etc, can help with that, while for the lunge, dumbbell lunges, kettlebell lunges, any kind of lunges will do as long as you are doing the movement, for the hinge, it is where you bend forward with maximal motion at the hips while keeping minimal motion of the knees, exercises such as the deadlift, romanian deadlift, single leg deadlift can be beneficial to this movement.

Summary

Functional training is just designed to make you ready for daily activities that require physical work, while it may also be not necessary to do this, it can certainly help with your training and give you the best that your body can offer.
