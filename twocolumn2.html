Training pillars of fitness

What kind of training are you doing right now?

Strength

Strength training has become the very staple of fitness, it is due to the required workload to lift more heavier weights which in return gives you a boost in endurance and strength, strength training also helps you to preserve and enhance muscle mass at any age, for strength training it is recommended to compound movements which consists of a push, pull, squat, hinge, and core, examples include bench press, bent rows, deadlifts, squats, overhead press, and etc, This should be done in low reps within 70-90RM% meaning this should be done within 70-90% off your effort and 4-6 reps so that you can reach muscle failure, it is recommended to do this 1-2x a week since recovery intervals between strength training is very long.

Endurance

Endurance training is the ability to exert and remain active for long periods of time, this can either be cardiovascular endurance aerobic endurance, anaerobic endurance, etc, Endurance training is often by bodyweight movement, such as explosive-push ups, explosive-pull-ups, jumping squats, etc, It can also be done on machines too such as the treadmill, stationary bike, battle ropes, and etc. It is recommended to do it at a pace where you won't fatigue easily since your goal is to stay in the activity as long as possible.

Agility

Agility training is a very broad spectrum due to how many factors there are that requires us to move efficiently such as sprint speed, jumping power, coordination, and much more. It is done by doing plyometrics or anything that has fast movement with it, It is usually done in a wide space due to you moving from another side to side, it can also be done in narrow places, sports such as basketball, football, and rugby are various types of agility training from sports, the goal is to move as quickly and efficiently.

Flexibility

Flexibility training is where you improve the muscle's elasticity which results in increased muscle control and range of motion, It is just basically stretching, activities such as yoga, tai chi, etc. It is done in a static position with a muscle being stretch at a slow pace.

Stability

One of the most underrated and not highly valued enough pillar of fitness, it is the ability to maintain equal equilibrium on both directions of the body, these kind of workouts require your core muscles and lower back to work together and it also works some of the muscles that are not targeted during any other exercises. It is often done on one part of the body usually hanging while the other part is doing the work to stabilize the body to keep it upright.
